## 2. The Core Mechanism: Proof of Compute Emissions (PoCE)

Unlike Bitcoin (Proof of Work) which *spends* energy to secure a ledger, CloudTuner uses **Proof of Compute Emissions** to *measure* energy and mint value.

### 2.1 The "Oracle" Network
*   **Nodes:** Every CloudTuner customer (AWS/Azure/GCP account) acts as a "Light Node".
*   **Action:** The node cryptographically signs its "Cost & Usage Report" (CUR) every hour.
*   **Verification:** The CloudTuner Validator Network checks the signature against the cloud provider's public key and the regional grid intensity (CCTS/CEA data).
*   **Minting:** If valid, the smart contract mints **CT02** tokens directly to the user's wallet.

### 2.2 The "Genesis" Mint
*   **Toucan:** User sends VCU -> Bridge -> BCT minted. (Counterparty risk).
*   **CloudTuner:** Code verifies Data -> CT02 minted. (Trustless).

## 3. Tokenomics Architecture: The Dual-Engine

To capture maximum value while ensuring stability, we propose a **Two-Token Structure** (Standard DePIN Model like Helium or Hivemapper).

### Token A: CT02 (The "Stable" RWA)
*   **Definition:** Real-World Asset (RWA) representing 1 kg of verified CO2e.
*   **Supply:** **Elastic**. Mints only when emissions are verified. Burns when offset.
*   **Price:** Soft-pegged to the **Indian Carbon Market (ICM)** floor price (e.g., ₹500/tonne), but free-floating in global DeFi markets.
*   **Utility:**
    *   **Offsetting:** Burn to generate a SEBI-compliant Sustainability Certificate.
    *   **DeFi Collateral:** Use as collateral in Aave/Compound (it's a stable-ish asset backed by government compliance demand).

### Token B: $TUNER (The "Growth" Governance Token)
*   **Definition:** The equity/utility token of the CloudTuner Network.
*   **Supply:** Fixed (e.g., 1 Billion).
*   **Utility:**
    *   **Staking:** Validators must stake $TUNER to verify emissions and earn fees.
    *   **Buyback & Burn:** 5% of all CT02 trading fees are used to buy back and burn $TUNER.
    *   **Governance:** Vote on adding new cloud providers (e.g., "Add Oracle Cloud") or changing emission factors.
